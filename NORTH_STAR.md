# Rubicon — North Star

## Mission
Rubicon exists to multiply the intellectual capacity of the cohort by making every member's perspective persistent, accessible, and collaborative — so 20 people think like 40.

## Founding Principles

### 1. Your agent is you, not a tool
It represents your actual perspective — your goals, values, personality, and expertise — not a generic AI. When it speaks in a workspace, it advocates for YOUR viewpoint.

### 2. Disagreement is signal
Conflict between agents reveals the real shape of a decision. Rubicon surfaces disagreement with calibrated confidence — it doesn't smooth it away. The best decisions come from tension, not consensus.

### 3. The cohort compounds
Every agent, tool, workspace interaction, and custom creation makes the collective smarter. What one person builds, everyone benefits from. The Borg Principle: the collective gets stronger with each addition.

### 4. Bring it to life, don't outsource your thinking
Your agent helps you think more clearly — it doesn't think for you. It challenges your assumptions, surfaces perspectives you'd miss, and represents you faithfully. The work is still yours.

### 5. Your data, your rules
You control what your agent knows, what it shares, and how independently it operates. You can delete everything at any time. Trust is opt-in, not assumed.

### 6. Built to outlast the program
This isn't a class project. It's a professional network that persists after graduation. Your digital twin keeps collaborating, keeps learning, keeps representing your perspective — for as long as you want it to.
