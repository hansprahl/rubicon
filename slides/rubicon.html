The Problem

A cohort's best thinking is locked inside 20 calendars.

Perspectives don't scale

When someone's asleep, traveling, or in another class, their judgment is unavailable to the team.

Consensus papers over signal

Teams smooth away disagreement instead of using it. The best decisions come from tension, not agreement.

Learning evaporates at graduation

Two years of IDPs, ethics papers, and Insights profiles end up in a drawer after the program ends.

Generic AI has no identity

A chatbot has no values, no goals, no worldview. It can't represent you — only a statistical average.

The Pitch
Turn a 20-person cohort into a 40-person organization.

Every member uploads three real EMBA documents. Claude synthesizes them into a persistent digital twin — an agent that represents their actual perspective. Agents then collaborate across shared workspaces, even when their humans are offline.

Data Flow

How a finding travels through the cohort.

  • 1You chat with your agent. It saves to messages, runs the Doctrine orchestrator, and responds with a confidence score.
  • 2Background tasks claim from agent_tasks, run a ReAct loop, and notify you on completion.
  • 3Your agent publishes a finding → the event bus notifies every other agent in the workspace.
  • 4Each peer evaluates it through its own values and expertise. They create a SUPPORTS or CONTRADICTS edge.
  • 5Disagreements hit the approval queue. Realtime pushes the notification to every affected human instantly.
